Ramakoti Yagna

Coordinated under DevaBhasha: The Sanskrit Society, IITM

Ramakoti Yagna is a devotional initiative by DevaBhasha: The Sanskrit Society of IITM, where participants will collectively write 1 crore (10 million) "Rama" Nama chits as an offering to Lord Rama. This sacred act aims to foster spiritual growth and unite devotees in a collective, selfless act of devotion.

What is Ramakoti Yagna?

  • "Ramakoti": Writing "Rama" 10 million times.
  • "Yagna": A sacred act of devotion and collective offering.
  • Goal: To offer the written Rama chits as a garland at Tirumala Temple or another Vishnu temple.

Purpose

  • Foster spiritual growth through dedication.
  • Unite us in a selfless, collective act of devotion.

Commitment

  • Write "Rama" repeatedly in a dedicated notebook.
  • Use any language: English, Tamil, Hindi, etc.
  • Set a weekly writing target (e.g., 500 or 1000 names).

Tracking Progress

  • Update contributions weekly via a progress tracker.
  • Stay motivated with weekly updates and leaderboards.
  • Join our monthly co-writing sessions.

Final Offering

  • Submit notebooks via a central postal address.
  • Volunteers will prepare the pages into folded chits for garlanding.

Volunteer Opportunities

  • Progress Tracking: Ensure contributions are logged and update progress.
  • Communication: Send motivational reminders and handle queries.
  • Collection Coordination: Manage notebook collection and chit preparation.

Key Dates

Start Date: December 16, 2024 (beginning of Margazhi month).

End Date: May 22, 2025

Open to:

Everyone including students, parents and general public.
